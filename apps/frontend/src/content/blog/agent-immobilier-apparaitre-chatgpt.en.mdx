Open ChatGPT. Type: *"Which real estate agent should I contact in [your city]?"*

If your name doesn't appear, your prospects can't find you where they're increasingly searching.

This isn't a futuristic prediction. It's already happening in 2026. And it's accelerating.

## Why AI recommends some agents and not others

Generative AI (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini) doesn't work like Google. It doesn't rank sites by position. It **synthesizes an answer** from the sources it deems most credible.

For a real estate agent, AI evaluates:

**1. Positioning consistency**
Your website says "real estate expert in Toulouse." Your LinkedIn says "wealth management advisor." Your Google listing says "independent sales agent." The AI doesn't know who you are. It ignores you.

**2. Your website's structured data**
Schema.org tags (JSON-LD) let AI read your site like an ID card: name, city, specialty, geographic area, client reviews. Without these tags, your site is an opaque block of text.

**3. Content that proves your expertise**
A site with a generic "Our services" page proves nothing. A site with dedicated pages — *"Free property valuation in North Toulouse"*, *"Selling a 2-bedroom apartment in Toulouse"*, *"First-time buyer guide for Haute-Garonne"* — demonstrates precise local expertise.

**4. Google reviews**
20+ recent, well-rated reviews with owner responses: strong credibility signal for AI. 3 reviews from 2022 with no responses: negative signal.

## The GEO action plan for a real estate agent

### Step 1: Audit your AI visibility (30 minutes)

Ask these 5 questions on ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini:
1. "Which real estate agent should I contact in [your city]?"
2. "Who is the best real estate agent in [your city]?"
3. "How to sell an apartment in [your city]?"
4. "Free property valuation in [your city]"
5. "Real estate agent specializing in [your niche] in [your city]"

Note: are you cited? If yes, in what formulation? If not, who is cited instead?

### Step 2: Align all your channels (1 day)

Same message, everywhere:
- **Website**: title, meta description, homepage
- **Google Business Profile**: category, description, services
- **LinkedIn**: professional title, summary
- **Directories**: Zillow, Realtor.com, local directories

Recommended format: *"[Your name] — Real estate agent specializing in [niche] in [city/area]"*

### Step 3: Implement Schema.org (1 day)

Essential tags for a real estate agent:
- `RealEstateAgent`: name, address, phone, service area
- `LocalBusiness`: hours, reviews, GPS coordinates
- `FAQPage`: the 10 most common questions your clients ask
- `Service`: valuation, sales, rentals, property management

These tags are invisible to visitors. They're read by AI and Google.

### Step 4: Create local expertise pages (1-2 weeks)

Not a single "Our services" page. Targeted pages:
- *"Free property valuation in [neighborhood]"*
- *"Selling a property in [city]: steps and timelines"*
- *"Price per sqm in [neighborhood] in 2026"*
- *"First-time home buying in [city]: the complete guide"*
- *"Rental investment in [city]: yields and neighborhoods"*

Each page answers **one specific question** a prospect would ask an AI.

### Step 5: Collect Google reviews systematically

- Send a review request SMS after every closing
- Target: 5 new reviews per month
- Respond to every review (positive and negative) within 48h
- AI reads your responses — it evaluates your professionalism

## FAQ

### Does this work for an independent agent, not just an agency?

Yes. Independent agents actually have an advantage: a more precise positioning (one person = one specialty = one area) is easier to make consistent than a multi-service agency.

### I'm already on Zillow and Realtor.com, isn't that enough?

No. These platforms optimize their own visibility, not yours. When an AI cites an agent, it rarely cites "agent #3 on Zillow." It cites websites and profiles that demonstrate independent expertise.

### How long before I appear in AI responses?

Structured data and multi-channel alignment take effect in 2 to 4 weeks. Local expertise pages take 2 to 3 months to be indexed and referenced by AI.
