Tuesday, 5:40 p.m. Mathieu is packing up his van on a site in Carmaux, an old mining town in the Tarn. He has just finished a full slate roof restoration on a turn-of-the-century town house — reinforced timber frame, new zinc work, clean valleys and flashings. Fifteen years in the trade, inherited from a father who taught him to read a roof frame the way others read a blueprint.

On the way home, he learns from a former client that he has lost a job in Lavaur. The prospect — a 180 m² clay-tile roof renovation — picked another roofer. Not because the other was cheaper. Not because he had better references. Just because "the other guy has a website where you can see his projects, and he called me back within the hour."

Mathieu has a phone book listing, a mobile number, and solid word of mouth around Carmaux and the Cérou valley. On his quote, like on every other quote in the region, three words: "free quote." Same as the competitor. Same as the 40 other roofers in the Tarn.

That’s exactly the problem. "Free quote" means nothing anymore — everyone says it. And when everyone says the same thing, nobody hears it.

## A market driven by renovation, but with increasingly demanding prospects

According to the FFB and the CAPEB, demand for roof renovation and energy-efficiency work keeps rising, pushed in particular by public renovation aid schemes such as MaPrimeRénov’. In practice: homeowners are more likely to take the plunge, and they are far better informed than they were ten years ago.

Better informed means they compare. A homeowner considering 15,000 to 30,000 € of roofing work no longer settles for a single quote. They ask for three, sometimes five. And between the first phone call and the signature, they spend hours online checking credentials, looking for photos, reading reviews, comparing guarantees. Your professional reputation is now built as much on a screen as on a building site.

Today’s prospect isn’t suspicious — they’re cautious. They want proof. And "free quote" isn’t proof.

## The hidden cost of looking like everyone else

Take Mathieu. He produces around 40 quotes a year on medium-to-large roof renovations (12,000 to 35,000 €). His win rate is around 35 %, which is decent. The remaining 65 % are prospects who went elsewhere.

Of those 26 lost quotes per year, how many were lost on price? Some, yes. But when he asks the prospects who were willing to explain, the same reasons come back: "We chose someone we trusted more," "The other one showed us photos of jobs like ours," "We didn’t really understand what you did exactly."

**Concrete calculation over a year:**

- 26 lost quotes, average basket 18,000 €
- If 5 out of those 26 could have been recovered with better presentation (not the ones chasing the lowest price, but the ones hesitating on trust)
- Average net margin on a roofing / zinc-work project: roughly 15 to 20 % depending on the mix
- 5 jobs × 18,000 € × 17 % ≈ **15,000 € of net margin left on the table every year** for competitors who communicate better without necessarily working better

This isn’t an official statistic — it’s basic arithmetic on a realistic recovery rate. And it’s more than enough to justify the conversation.

## What actually makes a difference in a prospect’s mind

Once "free quote" has become invisible, other signals take over. Here are the ones that really matter, in the order a prospect checks them:

1. **Visual proof of the work** — before/after photos of real roofs, not stock images. A homeowner who sees a reinforced roof frame, a clean zinc joint, a slate course laid to the string instantly grasps your level.
2. **Response time** — calling back within the hour or half a day, not two days later. Often the first filter a prospect uses.
3. **Displayed specialisations** — zinc, slate, canal clay tiles, flat tiles, traditional timber framing, factory trusses. A prospect with a local slate roof is looking for someone who knows slate, not a generalist.
4. **Guarantees and insurance** — up-to-date ten-year structural warranty, professional liability, and a clear note that these are verifiable.
5. **The RGE label** (Reconnu Garant de l’Environnement) where you hold it — mandatory for your customer to access MaPrimeRénov’ and the associated public aid. It’s a real differentiator, provided you put it front and centre.
6. **Named testimonials** — not "Mr D. in Albi" but a real first name, a real town, a real sentence. With the client’s consent, of course.
7. **Short site videos** — 30 seconds of zinc work being laid or a roof frame going up is worth ten paragraphs of text.

None of this invents your expertise. It simply makes it legible to someone who doesn’t know you yet.

## The 3 levels of solution for a roofer-carpenter

### Level 1 — Single pro page with gallery and form: free to 300 €

A single well-built page is already a huge leap from a phone-directory listing. The goal: a prospect landing on it should understand in 30 seconds what you do, for whom, and be able to request a quote without calling.

**What it does:**
- Before/after photo gallery organised by project type (slate, tile, zinc, timber frame)
- Clear specialisations section
- Quote request form with the option for the prospect to upload a roof photo
- Ten-year warranty, public liability, RGE where applicable
- Basic local SEO on terms like "roofer Carmaux", "carpenter Lavaur", etc.

**What it doesn’t do:**
- No blog or dedicated project pages — little long-term SEO content
- No structured testimonials or detailed case pages
- Limited scalability: when you want to expand, you often have to rebuild

**For whom:** a sole trader or two-person outfit who works mostly on local word of mouth and just wants to stop losing quotes due to online invisibility.

**Setup:** 2 to 4 days. Platforms like Carrd, or a simple HTML/CSS site on low-cost hosting. Form via a service like Formspree.

### Level 2 — Full site with project pages, testimonials and RGE showcase: 2,000 to 4,000 €

The right level for a tradesperson or small firm (2 to 6 workers) who wants a real conversion tool. The idea: give the prospect every reason to choose you before they even pick up the phone.

**What it does:**
- A homepage highlighting your specialisations (slate, zinc, clay tiles, timber framing)
- Dedicated pages per project type with before/after photos, technical description, duration, location (town), and client testimonial where available
- A "guarantees and labels" page explaining the ten-year warranty, PL insurance, and the RGE label if held (with verifiable number)
- A testimonial module with client first name, town, project type
- An enriched quote form (roof type, estimated area, urgency, photos)
- Careful local SEO across towns in your area (Carmaux, Lavaur, Graulhet, Mazamet, Réalmont, etc.)
- Live Google or directory reviews embedded

**What it doesn’t do:**
- No CRM or automated quote tracking — you still manage them the old way
- No integration with a trade estimating tool
- Videos, if any, are hosted on YouTube or Vimeo, not a custom player

**For whom:** an established roofer-zinc worker or carpenter doing 30 to 80 projects a year, who wants to stand out within 30 km of their base.

**Setup:** 2 to 4 weeks, a good chunk of it spent collecting and sorting your site photos. That’s where the project takes time — not in the code.

### Level 3 — Custom site with CRM, marketing automation and pre-qualified quotes: 5,000 to 9,000 € then 30 to 60 €/month

The level for a building firm that wants to industrialise its conversion. The goal: prospects arriving on the site are already pre-qualified by the time they hit your calendar, so you never waste a minute on off-target enquiries.

**What it does:**
- Everything in Level 2
- A smart qualification questionnaire (roof type, area, urgency, indicative budget, MaPrimeRénov’ eligibility)
- A client area where the prospect can upload photos, track their quote, sign online
- An integrated CRM to follow every request (automatic follow-up at D+3, D+7, D+15)
- An automated newsletter to stay top of mind with past clients (annual roof check reminder, for instance)
- A dashboard with your stats: number of requests, conversion rate, lead source
- Town-by-town pages for deeper local SEO

**What it doesn’t do:**
- Doesn’t maintain itself: content needs feeding, enquiries need answering, the process needs respecting
- ROI visible over 12 to 18 months, not three weeks
- Oversized if you do fewer than 40 jobs a year

**For whom:** a structured building firm with 4 to 15 workers that wants to move from passive word of mouth to a qualified-quote machine.

**Setup:** 6 to 10 weeks. Training included so you or your assistant can run the tool.

## Traps to avoid

**Trap 1 — Using stock photography.** A prospect spots a generic tiled-roof image in three seconds. At that moment they think: "if he has no photos of his own, he must not have real projects to show." Five average photos of your real jobs beat a polished gallery of bought visuals.

**Trap 2 — Mentioning RGE without detailing it.** If you hold the label, name the certifying body (for example Qualibat for building trades, or Qualit’EnR for renewable energy where relevant) and state the applicable category clearly. A vague RGE mention is almost as bad as none: informed prospects verify.

**Trap 3 — Not displaying a response time.** If you typically call back within 24 hours, say so. "We call you back within 24 working hours" is a simple commitment that reassures — provided you keep it. It’s far more powerful than "free quote".

**Trap 4 — Outsourcing to someone with zero building-trade knowledge.** A developer who has never seen a roof will propose a generic "home services" template with round icons. You need someone who understands a roofing job is not a painting job, and that a photo of a clean zinc flashing is worth as much as a written testimonial.

## Three questions to ask yourself this weekend

1. **How many quotes did I lose last year without really knowing why?** If you don’t track the reason behind each "no", you’re flying blind. A single line per lost quote is enough to reveal patterns.

2. **What does a prospect see of me when they type my name on Google?** Open a private window, type your name or "roofer + your town", and look honestly. That’s what the prospect sees — and it’s often far below the reality of your craft.

3. **What are the three things I do better than other roofers in my area?** Pure-tin zinc work? Traditional timber framing? Local slate? Thatch? If you have a clear answer, your site should start there. If you don’t, that’s the first site to build — in your head.
