Your phone rings. You're in the middle of service, hands literally in the sauce. It's a reservation for Saturday evening, 4 people, 8:30 PM. You scribble it on a scrap of paper. You hang up. It rings again. Same scenario. Meanwhile, a customer in the dining room is waiting for their dish.

## The real cost of the phone in a restaurant

Let's take a 40-seat restaurant, lunch and dinner service, 6 days a week.

**Time spent on the phone for reservations:** between 1.5 and 2.5 hours per day. The observed average among independent restaurant owners hovers around 2 hours.

The math is straightforward:

| Data point | Value |
|------------|-------|
| Phone time/day | 2h |
| Loaded hourly cost (employee or owner) | €23 to €28/h |
| Daily cost | €46 to €56 |
| Monthly cost (26 days) | €1,196 to €1,456 |
| Annual cost | €13,870 to €16,900 |

And this doesn't account for missed calls. A restaurant without online reservations loses an average of 12 to 18% of reservation requests — people call, nobody picks up, they go elsewhere.

## The free bonus: Google Reserve + Instagram

Two channels often ignored by restaurant owners — and they're both free.

### Google Reserve (via Google Maps)

If your Google Business profile is up to date, you can enable reservations directly from Google Maps and Google Search. A customer types "Italian restaurant Albi," sees your listing, and books without leaving Google.

**Prerequisite:** you need to be partnered with a compatible platform (Zenchef, TheFork, Resmio are integrated). Google doesn't handle reservations itself — it redirects to your platform.

**Cost:** €0 extra if you already have a compatible platform.

### "Reserve" button on Instagram

Since 2024, Instagram lets you add a "Reserve" action button on your professional profile. It redirects to your booking link (website, platform, or WhatsApp).

**Setup:** Professional profile → Edit profile → Action buttons → Reserve → Your reservation page URL.

**Cost:** €0.

These two channels don't replace a reservation system, but they multiply entry points. A customer who finds you on Google or Instagram can book without searching for your phone number.

## FAQ

### How do I handle no-shows with an automated system?

Three combined mechanisms reduce no-shows by 35 to 60%:
1. **Automatic D-1 reminder** via SMS or WhatsApp with a confirm/cancel button
2. **Credit card pre-authorization** (offered by Zenchef, Guestonline): the customer enters their card, you charge a flat fee per no-show (€5 to €15 per person, at your discretion)
3. **Automatic waitlist**: when a customer cancels, the next person on the list gets notified

No-shows remain a problem in the restaurant industry (average rate of 15 to 20% without countermeasures), but automation reduces them significantly compared to traditional phone reservations with no reminders.

### Does it work during peak hours?

That's precisely when it works best. During the rush (Friday evening, Saturday evening, Sunday lunch service), the phone is unmanageable. The automated system keeps taking reservations while you're in the kitchen.

An important point: configure limits per time slot. If your dining room seats 40 and you run two sittings (7:30 PM and 9:30 PM), cap at 20 covers per slot. The system automatically declines when full — no more manual checking.

### Will my 65+ year-old customers use an online system?

This question comes up every time. Here are the facts: in 2026, 68% of people aged 60-75 in France use a smartphone, and 53% of them make purchases or reservations online (source: Baromètre du numérique 2025, Arcep/Credoc).

The online system doesn't replace the phone — it complements it. Keep your number visible on your Google listing and your website. Customers who prefer calling will keep calling. The rest (the majority) book online, freeing up your time for those who need human contact.

### Is TheFork worth it with its commissions?

TheFork brings visibility — that's undeniable. If your restaurant just opened or is short on customers, TheFork's user base (approximately 38 million in Europe) is an acquisition lever.

But run the numbers for your actual situation. A 40-seat restaurant, 78% fill rate, 2 services per day, 6 days a week, with an average TheFork commission of €2.50 per cover:

- Daily covers via TheFork (estimated 40% of total): 25 covers
- Daily commission: €62.40
- Monthly commission: €1,622
- Annual commission: €18,878

If you already have loyal regulars and strong local visibility, Zenchef or a custom chatbot without commissions will be more profitable in the medium term. If you're just starting out, TheFork remains an accelerator — but plan to migrate to a commission-free solution once your customer base is established.
