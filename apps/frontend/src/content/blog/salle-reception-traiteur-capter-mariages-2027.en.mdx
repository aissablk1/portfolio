Saturday morning, 9:15 a.m. Julien and Anaïs are standing in the courtyard of their estate near Castelnau-de-Montmiral. They have just hung up with a couple from Toulouse looking for a venue for August 2027. Third call this week. The previous couple, who contacted them on Monday, never called back — Julien took three days to reply to the form received on their old Facebook page, and by then a neighbouring estate had sent a quote the same evening. Booking lost.

Julien and Anaïs host 18 to 22 weddings per season, from April to October. Their 2026 calendar has been full since last September. But 2027 is still almost a blank page — and it’s right now, in spring 2026, that couples are making their choice. Enquiries land on their Facebook page, by email, sometimes by text via a mutual friend. They reply when they can, between two services and an appointment with the florist.

They know they’re leaving bookings on the table. They don’t know how many.

## Why 2027 weddings are being decided now

The French wedding booking cycle is long: between **12 and 18 months** from the first venue search to the big day, sometimes more for the most in-demand estates. A couple getting married in June 2027 starts visiting venues in March or April 2026, locks in their choice by May or June, and signs the contract soon after. Sector studies published by French wedding portals confirm this window: most venue bookings happen 12 to 15 months before the event.

In other words: **the 2027 prospecting season plays out between March and July 2026**. Every week your venue isn’t visible, or where your replies take more than 24 hours, is a lost booking. And unlike a restaurant that can fill a table within the week, a lost Saturday in June can’t be recovered — it only comes back next year.

Seasonality makes the stakes even sharper. A venue operating from April to October has **28 workable Saturdays a year**. At 8,000 to 15,000 € per Saturday depending on the package, each slot literally represents a month’s worth of salary.

## The hidden cost of an enquiry that goes to the competitor

Take a venue that receives 120 wedding enquiries a year — a common order of magnitude for a venue listed on one or two wedding portals and active on Instagram. Here is what actually happens with the providers I meet:

- **40 %** don’t get a reply within 24 hours → half go to a competitor
- **25 %** get a reply but no itemised quote → a third give up
- **15 %** get a quote but haven’t seen convincing photos → low conversion
- **20 %** make it to the on-site visit → high signing rate

In the end, out of 120 enquiries, a poorly equipped venue signs **10 to 15 weddings a year**. A well-equipped venue — instant auto-reply, clear photo gallery, semi-automated quote, scheduled follow-ups — signs **22 to 28 weddings** on the exact same inbound volume. The difference is not in traffic, it’s in **conversion**.

**Concrete calculation for a venue with an average ticket of 10,000 €:**

- Current scenario: 12 weddings × 10,000 € = **120,000 € annual revenue**
- Optimised scenario: 24 weddings × 10,000 € = **240,000 € annual revenue**
- Difference: **+120,000 €/year**, without spending a single extra euro on advertising

This isn’t magic. It’s what you get from a website that answers at 3 a.m., a form that qualifies the request (date, budget, guest count), and a workflow that sends an indicative first quote within the hour.

## The 3 levels of solution

### Level 1 — Landing page + qualified form: 0 to 300 €

The minimum viable setup: a single page with 8 to 12 great photos of the venue, an honest description (capacity, seasons, equipment), the included services, and a form that collects desired date, guest count, rough budget, contact details. Instant automatic email reply ("we’ve received your request, Julien will get back to you within 24 hours").

**What it does:**
- Captures enquiries 24/7, including weekends and evenings
- Qualifies the request before any human contact
- Sends an immediate acknowledgement that reassures the couple ("they received it, they’ll reply")
- Builds an email list of interested prospects

**What it doesn’t do:**
- No automatic quote — you write each reply by hand
- No live availability calendar — you check manually
- No automatic follow-up if the couple doesn’t respond to the quote

**For whom:** a venue just entering the wedding market, or a caterer who wants to test demand without investing. Also for venues already running at full capacity who simply want to stop losing enquiries between two services.

**Setup:** 2 to 4 days. A page on Carrd (19 €/year), a form via Tally or Formspree (free up to 50 submissions/month), a domain name (12 €/year). Total first year: under 50 €.

### Level 2 — Showcase site + gallery + semi-automated quote: 1,500 to 4,000 €

The right level for an established venue that wants to professionalise its presence. A 5 to 8 page site built on WordPress or a modern stack (Next.js, Astro), with a gallery organised by theme (civil ceremony, dinner, party, next-day brunch), dedicated pages for recommended vendors, verifiable customer reviews, and a form connected to a tool like Calendly or Cal.com to book a site visit directly.

**What it does:**
- Everything in Level 1, better presented and more credible
- Live availability calendar (or on-request)
- Direct visit booking from the site
- Pre-filled indicative quote generated from the form answers
- SEO pages ("Tarn wedding venue", "Gaillac wedding estate") that capture Google traffic without ads
- Integration with the Mariages.net and Zankyou portals (enriched listings pointing back to your site)

**What it doesn’t do:**
- No full CRM: follow-ups stay manual
- No immersive virtual tour (simple 360° photos possible, no real 3D)
- Doesn’t replace Instagram and Pinterest — it complements them

**For whom:** a venue hosting 10 to 30 weddings a year that wants to grow to 20 to 40, or a caterer who wants to capture couples directly without relying only on portals.

**Setup:** 3 to 6 weeks. Budget 1,500 to 4,000 € for the build, then 15 to 30 €/month (hosting + domain + booking tool).

### Level 3 — Custom site + virtual tour + CRM: 5,000 to 12,000 € then 40 to 80 €/month

The option for venues that want to treat their wedding business as a real business. Fully custom site, 360° virtual tour or immersive drone video, integrated CRM that tracks every prospect from first enquiry to signature, scheduled automatic follow-ups (D+2, D+7, D+21 if no response), dashboard with conversion rate per source (Instagram, Pinterest, portal, word-of-mouth), PDF quote and contract generation from the back office.

**What it does:**
- Unique design that tells the story of the venue — not a template that looks like the 50 other estates in the region
- Navigable virtual tour: the couple in Toulouse or Bordeaux feels the atmosphere before travelling
- Full CRM: you always know how many prospects are in the pipeline, at what stage, and what revenue they represent
- Automatic follow-ups: a couple who requests a quote in March 2026 and doesn’t reply gets a gentle reminder in April, then in May
- Seasonal dashboard: you see at a glance the occupancy rate for 2027, 2028, and the weekends still available
- Email marketing integration (newsletter to past clients for anniversaries, or to vendor partners)

**What it doesn’t do:**
- Doesn’t maintain itself: you need to feed in photos, keep the calendar current, handle the follow-ups
- ROI over 12 to 24 months, not instant
- Not suited for a venue hosting fewer than 8 weddings a year — too expensive for the volume

**For whom:** ambitious venues aiming for 25 to 50 weddings per season, multi-space event châteaux, or high-end caterers who want to structure their pipeline and stop losing enquiries in a paper notebook.

**Setup:** 6 to 12 weeks. Design 5,000 to 12,000 € depending on complexity (virtual tour extra if shot by a specialist, budget 800 to 2,000 €), then 40 to 80 €/month for hosting, CRM, and email tools.

## Traps to avoid

**Trap 1 — Relying entirely on wedding portals.** Mariages.net and Zankyou are unavoidable for visibility — but you’re drowning in 40 to 80 competing venues in your département, and the listing isn’t yours. You pay an annual subscription with no volume guarantee, and you don’t control the content that appears. Portals are an **acquisition channel**, not a substitute for your own site. Use them to pull in traffic, then bring prospects back home.

**Trap 2 — Neglecting Pinterest.** Pinterest is the first reflex of brides-to-be when building their moodboard — often 12 to 18 months before the wedding, exactly the window where you want to be seen. A venue without an active Pinterest account is missing out on a free, highly intent-driven channel. Instagram is useful for nurturing the relationship, but Pinterest is the number-one discovery tool in this space.

**Trap 3 — Replying in more than 24 hours.** The first to reply with a clear quote wins the site visit 6 times out of 10. If your reply takes 48 or 72 hours, you’re already out of the race, no matter how beautiful your venue is. That’s why the instant auto-reply of Level 1 is often more decisive than the perfect design of Level 3.

**Trap 4 — Inconsistent amateur photos.** A site with 40 smartphone photos in random lighting sends an "amateur" signal, even if the venue itself is stunning. Ten professional photos beat an incoherent catalogue every time. A dedicated shoot for presentation (off-wedding, morning light, styled) costs 400 to 900 € and pays off on a single additional booking.

## Three questions to ask yourself this weekend

1. **How many wedding enquiries did I receive last month, and how many did I convert into site visits?** If you can’t answer precisely, that’s already the first problem. You can’t steer what you don’t measure — and without measurement, it’s impossible to know where you’re losing prospects.
