A coach publishes their online course, creates a sales page, shares the link on Instagram and LinkedIn. Result: 1 to 2% conversion. Out of 1,000 visitors, 10 to 20 buy. The other 980 leave without a trace.

This isn't a course quality problem. It's a sales architecture problem.

A standalone sales page asks the visitor to go from "I'm just looking" to "here's my credit card" in a single step. It's like proposing marriage on the first date. It can happen, but it's statistically rare.

## Standalone sales page vs sales funnel: the numbers

| Indicator | Standalone sales page | Complete sales funnel |
|---|---|---|
| **Conversion rate visitor → purchase** | 1 to 2% | 4 to 8% |
| **Customer acquisition cost** (ads at €3/click) | €150 to €300 | €37 to €75 |
| **Average order value** | Course price only | +18 to 35% (upsell + order bump) |
| **Recurring revenue** | None | Post-purchase sequence → other offers |
| **Data collected on non-buyers** | 0 | Email from 25 to 40% of visitors |

Conversion rates vary by niche, course price, and traffic quality. These ranges are observed on courses priced between €97 and €497, with organic traffic (social media, SEO) and paid traffic (Meta Ads, Google Ads).

## The 5 funnel stages

### Stage 1 — The lead magnet (the hook)

The lead magnet is a free piece of content exchanged for the visitor's email address. It's the only stage that converts curious visitors into actionable contacts.

**What works in coaching/courses:**
- A 3-day mini-course by email (opt-in rate: 25 to 40%)
- An 8 to 15-page PDF guide on a specific problem (opt-in rate: 15 to 30%)
- A self-assessment quiz with a personalized result (opt-in rate: 30 to 50%)
- A 20-minute video excerpt from the full course (opt-in rate: 20 to 35%)

The quiz is the top-performing format in coaching. The visitor answers 7-10 questions, gets a profile ("You're at 38% of your time management potential"), and receives a personalized action plan by email. Emotional engagement is at its peak.

**The fatal mistake:** a lead magnet that's too generic. "10 Keys to Well-Being" converts nobody. "The 5-Day Action Plan to Stop Procrastinating After Work" converts people who have exactly that problem.

### Stage 2 — The capture page

The capture page has one single objective: collect the email. No navigation menu, no outgoing links, no distractions. A headline, a promise, a form.

**Structure that converts:**
1. Result-oriented headline: "Discover your productivity profile in 3 minutes"
2. 3 to 4 bullet points about what the visitor will get
3. Form: first name + email (two fields maximum)
4. Specific action button: "Get my action plan" (not "Sign up" — too vague)

**Average conversion rate of a well-built capture page:** 25 to 45%. If you're below 20%, your promise isn't specific enough or your traffic isn't qualified.

### Stage 3 — The 5-email sequence

This is the funnel's engine. 5 emails sent over 7 to 10 days, each with a specific role.

| Email | Day | Objective | Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Email 1** | D+0 | Deliver the lead magnet | Download link + short introduction about you |
| **Email 2** | D+1 | Tell your story | How you solved the problem your client has today |
| **Email 3** | D+3 | Deliver value | An actionable tip the prospect can apply immediately |
| **Email 4** | D+5 | Handle objections | Answer the 3 reasons people don't buy |
| **Email 5** | D+7 | Open the sale | Course presentation + sales page link + time-limited bonus |

**Email 4 is the most underestimated.** The 3 universal objections in online courses are: "I don't have time," "it's too expensive," "it won't work for me." If your email 4 doesn't address these three points with concrete proof (testimonials, case studies, guarantee), you lose 30 to 40% of potential sales.

**Target open rate:** 35 to 55% for emails 1 and 2, 25 to 40% for emails 3 to 5. If you're below that, your subject lines aren't specific enough.

### Stage 4 — The sales page

The sales page no longer addresses strangers. It addresses people who read your lead magnet, opened 3 to 5 of your emails, and are already convinced you understand their problem. The persuasion work is 70% done. The sales page closes.

**8-block structure:**
1. **Hook** — restate the prospect's problem (not your solution)
2. **Agitation** — the consequences of changing nothing
3. **Transformation** — the concrete result after the course (before/after)
4. **Curriculum** — the modules, content, duration
5. **Social proof** — testimonials, numbered results from past clients
6. **Price + guarantee** — clear price, 30-day money-back guarantee
7. **FAQ** — address remaining objections
8. **Final CTA** — buy button + guarantee reminder

### Stage 5 — The upsell and order bump

The upsell and order bump are the two levers that increase average order value without increasing acquisition cost.

**Order bump** — a checkbox on the payment page: "Add the ready-to-use templates for €27." Average acceptance rate: 22 to 38%. It's a low-cost complementary product that requires no additional thinking.

**Upsell** — an intermediate page between payment and confirmation: "You just purchased course X. For €97 more, access 3 individual coaching sessions." Average acceptance rate: 8 to 15%.

On a €297 course with a €27 order bump (30% rate) and a €97 upsell (12% rate), the average order value goes from €297 to **€316** — a +6.4% revenue increase with zero additional ad spend.

## FAQ

### How long does it take to build a complete sales funnel?

With Systeme.io or Podia, plan 5 to 7 effective working days: 1 day for the lead magnet, 1 day for the capture page and sales page, 2 to 3 days for the 5-email sequence, 1 day for the upsell/order bump and testing. The longest part is writing the emails — that's what makes the difference between a funnel that converts at 2% and a funnel that converts at 7%.

### Does a sales funnel work without paid advertising?

Yes, as long as you have a traffic source. A coach with 2,000 LinkedIn followers who posts 3 times a week can send 200 to 400 visitors per month to their funnel. With a 5% conversion rate, that's 10 to 20 sales per month. Advertising accelerates volume, but it's not essential when starting out.

### Do I need a money-back guarantee?

Yes. The 30-day guarantee is standard for online courses. It reduces purchase friction by 18 to 25% according to A/B tests from my clients. The actual refund rate is 3 to 8% — more than offset by the increase in sales. A prospect hesitating between buying and not buying needs to know they can back out.

### What's the difference between a Systeme.io funnel and a custom-built funnel?

The Systeme.io funnel is a customized template: you pick a model, fill in your text and images, connect your payment method. The custom-built funnel is an architecture designed specifically for your offer: unique design, purchase flow tailored to your audience, integration with your existing tools (CRM, email, accounting), and zero dependency on a platform. Custom-built makes sense when your course exceeds €5,000 in monthly revenue and you want distinctive professional branding.
