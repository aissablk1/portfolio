You're losing time on repetitive tasks: sending follow-up emails, copying data between tools, chasing prospects, creating invoices. You know it can be automated.

But which tool to choose? n8n, Make, and Zapier are the 3 leaders. Each has its strengths and limitations. Here's the objective comparison.

## Quick overview

| | n8n | Make | Zapier |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Model** | Open source (self-hosted or cloud) | Cloud | Cloud |
| **Entry price** | Free (self-hosted) / €24/month (cloud) | Free (limited) / €10.59/month | Free (limited) / $29.99/month |
| **Complexity** | Intermediate to advanced | Beginner to intermediate | Beginner |
| **Integrations** | 400+ | 1,500+ | 7,000+ |
| **Ideal for** | Tech-savvy, complex workflows | SMBs, visual workflows | Non-tech, simple automations |

## Zapier: simplest, most expensive

### Strengths
- **7,000+ integrations**: the largest catalog. If your tool exists, Zapier probably supports it.
- **Ultra-simple interface**: "when this happens → do that." No technical skills needed.
- **Reliability**: 99.9% uptime. Automations run unsupervised.

### Limitations
- **Prices that explode**: the free plan is limited to 100 tasks/month (unusable). Paid plans start at $29.99/month for 750 tasks. An active SMB easily reaches 2,000 to 5,000 tasks/month → $73 to $193/month.
- **Linear workflows**: Zapier works in simple chains (A → B → C). Workflows with complex conditions, loops, or parallel branches are difficult or impossible.
- **No self-hosting**: your data goes through Zapier's servers. For companies with strict GDPR constraints, that's a problem.

### Zapier verdict
**Choose Zapier if** you want to connect 2-3 tools with simple automations and have zero technical skills. Accept that the price will increase with your usage.

## Make: best value for money

### Strengths
- **Powerful visual interface**: workflows are visual diagrams with connected modules. Much more readable than Zapier for complex scenarios.
- **Aggressive pricing**: €10.59/month for 10,000 operations. That's 5 to 10x cheaper than Zapier at comparable volume.
- **Advanced workflows**: conditional branches, loops, error handling, routers — Make handles the complexity Zapier can't.
- **1,500+ integrations**: fewer than Zapier, but covers all major tools.

### Limitations
- **Learning curve**: the visual interface is powerful but more complex than Zapier. Plan 2 to 4 hours to master the basics.
- **No self-hosting**: like Zapier, your data goes through Make's servers.
- **Limited support on small plans**: priority support is only available from the Teams plan (€184.17/month).

### Make verdict
**Choose Make if** you have moderately complex workflows (5 to 15 steps), a limited budget, and are willing to invest a few hours learning. It's the sweet spot for most SMBs.

## n8n: most powerful, most technical

### Strengths
- **Open source and self-hosted**: you can host n8n on your own server. Your data never leaves your infrastructure. Full GDPR compliance.
- **Free when self-hosted**: the cost is limited to hosting (€5 to €20/month on a VPS).
- **Unlimited power**: n8n can execute JavaScript/Python code in workflows, call any API, handle 100+ node workflows.
- **Active community**: 400+ official integrations + thousands of community-shared workflows.

### Limitations
- **Requires technical skills**: installing, configuring, and maintaining an n8n server requires DevOps knowledge. It's not a no-code tool in the strict sense.
- **Fewer native integrations**: 400+ versus 7,000+ for Zapier. Missing integrations can be created manually via API, but it takes work.
- **Recent cloud version**: n8n Cloud exists (from €24/month) but is less mature than Make or Zapier in terms of cloud UX.

### n8n verdict
**Choose n8n if** you have technical skills (or a developer), strong GDPR constraints, complex workflows, or a very limited budget. It's the most powerful tool, but it requires skills investment.

## My choice for my clients

For the SMBs I work with, I mainly use **n8n** (self-hosted) and **Make**.

- **n8n** when the client has GDPR constraints, complex workflows, or when I manage the infrastructure myself as part of a package.
- **Make** when the client wants to be autonomous with their automations and complexity is moderate.
- **Zapier** only for simple connections between 2 SaaS tools, when the client refuses to learn Make.

The choice always depends on context. There's no "best" tool — there's the tool adapted to your situation.

## FAQ

### Can I migrate from Zapier to Make or n8n?

Yes, but there's no automatic migration. Workflows must be recreated manually. Plan 1 to 2 hours per simple workflow, 4 to 8 hours for a complex one.

### Which one do you recommend for a contractor with no technical skills?

Make with initial guidance. 2 hours of training is enough to be autonomous on simple scenarios (new lead notification, automatic quote sending, email follow-up).

### Can these tools replace a CRM?

No. They're automation tools, not CRMs. They connect and automate your existing tools. However, they can make a basic CRM (like a simple Notion table or Google Sheets) much more powerful by automating the flows around it.
