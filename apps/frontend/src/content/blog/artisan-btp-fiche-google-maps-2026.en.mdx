A client searches "mason near me" on their phone. Google shows 3 listings. The first has 47 reviews and a 4.8 rating, with real job site photos. The second has 12 reviews. The third has no photos at all.

The client calls the first one. The other two will never know he existed.

87% of consumers use Google to search for a local professional (source: BrightLocal, Local Consumer Review Survey 2025). For a contractor, not being in the top 3 on Google Maps means not existing for the majority of your future clients.

## Why Google Maps is now the first reflex for construction clients

Word of mouth used to be enough. A neighbor needed a plumber, they asked the neighbor next door. That era isn't over, but it now has a mandatory complement: Google.

The numbers are clear:

- 46% of Google searches have local intent (source: GoGulf, 2024)
- 76% of people who search for a local service on their phone visit a business within 24 hours (source: Google/Ipsos, Think with Google)
- The "Local Pack" (the 3 listings displayed at the top of Google Maps) captures 42% of clicks on the results page (source: Moz, Local Search Ranking Factors)

When someone types "electrician near me" or "plumber in [your city]," Google shows Google Business Profile (GBP) listings first. Not websites. Not directories. GBP listings.

If yours isn't optimized, you're invisible.

## The 7 steps to dominate Google Maps in your area

### Step 1: Create or claim your Google Business Profile

If you don't have a listing, go to [business.google.com](https://business.google.com). It's free to create. Google will send a verification code by postal mail (5 to 14 days) or by phone call.

If a listing already exists with your name (a former employee created it, or Google generated it automatically), claim it. Click "Own this business?" on the listing and follow the verification steps.

**Common trap:** Many contractors create a listing and never touch it again. A listing created but not optimized is worse than no listing at all — it gives an impression of abandonment.

### Step 2: Fill in every field precisely

Google ranks listings based on relevance, distance, and prominence. Relevance depends directly on how complete your listing is.

Critical fields:

- **Business name**: your real name, not "John Smith Masonry Albi Tarn 81" (Google penalizes keyword stuffing in the name)
- **Primary category**: choose the most precise one. "Mason" is better than "Construction company." Add secondary categories: "Renovation contractor," "Landscaper"
- **Service area**: list each city and town you serve. Don't just put "the county" — list specific locations
- **Hours**: put your real hours, including special hours (holidays, annual leave)
- **Description**: 750 characters to describe your business. Include your specialties, your geographic area, your experience. Write for a human, not a robot

### Step 3: Add photos from your job sites — real ones

Listings with more than 100 photos receive 520% more calls than the average listing and 2,717% more direction requests (source: BrightLocal, Google Business Profile Insights Study 2023).

No photographer needed. Your phone is enough. What to post:

- **Before/after** of each job (the most convincing)
- Photos **during the work** (shows professionalism)
- Photo of the team on site (humanizes the business)
- Photo of your vehicles with the logo (strengthens the brand)
- Photos of materials and finishes (shows attention to detail)

**Frequency:** 2 to 4 photos per week. Google favors active, consistently updated listings.

### Step 4: Collect reviews — systematically

A contractor with 47 reviews and a 4.7 rating will always be chosen over a contractor with 3 reviews and a perfect 5.0. Volume matters as much as the rating.

How to collect without being pushy:

- **Text after the job**: send a text message with the direct link to your Google review page. The link is generated from your GBP dashboard (Marketing > Ask for reviews)
- **QR code on your invoice**: a QR code linking to your review page
- **Ask in person**: "If you're happy with the work, a Google review would really help me out. It takes 2 minutes." Honest, direct, no pressure

**Realistic target:** 2 to 3 reviews per month. In one year, you have 24 to 36 reviews. That's more than enough to dominate a local area.

**Reply to every review.** Positive: thank them and mention the type of job ("Thanks Marc, glad you're happy with the bathroom renovation"). Negative: stay factual, offer to resolve privately. Google values listings where the owner is responsive.

### Step 5: Post Google updates regularly

Few contractors know this: your GBP listing lets you publish posts (like a mini social network). These posts appear directly on your listing in Google Maps.

Effective post types:

- **Completed job**: photo + short description ("Complete roof renovation in [city] — clay tiles, 1,940 sq ft")
- **Seasonal offer**: "Free quote for your excavation work before winter — response within 48 hours"
- **Industry news**: "New energy regulations: what changes for your building projects in 2026"

**Frequency:** 1 post per week minimum. Each post stays visible for 7 days.

### Step 6: Ensure NAP consistency

NAP = Name, Address, Phone. These 3 pieces of information must be strictly identical everywhere: GBP listing, website, directories, industry listings, Facebook page.

If your Google listing says "12 Main Street, Suite 3, Springfield" and your website says "12 Main St, Springfield," Google may treat them as potentially different businesses. That dilutes your authority.

Do a quick audit: search your business name on Google and check every mention. Fix any inconsistencies.

### Step 7: Link your listing to a real website

A GBP listing without a website can work. But a listing with an optimized website performs significantly better.

The site doesn't need to be complex. A homepage with your services, a page for each type of work, a contact page with a form. The key: the site should confirm and expand on the information in your GBP listing.

The link from your listing to your site (and vice versa, with a Google Maps embed on your contact page) creates a trust signal that Google uses to rank you.

## FAQ

### How long before seeing results?

Expect 3 to 6 months for significant results. Early effects (more views on your listing) often show within the first 4 weeks if you post photos and collect reviews. Ranking in the top 3 depends on local competition: in a town of 50,000 people, it happens faster than in a major city.

### Can you show up on Google Maps without a website?

Yes. The Google Business Profile works independently. You'll appear in Maps results and in the Local Pack. However, without a website, you lose an important trust signal for Google and you miss out on organic search results (the results below the Local Pack).

### Do you need to pay Google to rank better on Maps?

No. Organic ranking on Google Maps is entirely free. Google offers "Google Ads for Maps" (paid local ads), but your organic ranking depends solely on the quality of your listing, your reviews, and your NAP consistency. Paying for Google Ads can complement your efforts, but it's never a prerequisite.

### How many reviews do you need to be credible?

The psychological threshold is around 10 to 15 reviews with a rating above 4.2. Below 10 reviews, clients hesitate — they suspect the reviews are fake or that the business is brand new. Above 30 reviews, the trust effect is at its peak. The priority: reach 15 reviews within the first 3 months.
