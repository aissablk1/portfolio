Your accounting firm is running. Clients keep coming, files keep piling up, deadlines keep falling. Your staff spends their days entering invoices, chasing late payments, reconciling bank statements.

What if 60% of that work could run on autopilot?

## The time lost on repetitive tasks in an accounting firm

According to a 2025 study by the French Institute of Chartered Accountants, an accounting staff member spends an average of **40% of their time** on repetitive, automatable tasks: data entry, reconciliation, payment reminders, generating standardised documents.

Let's do the maths. A staff member at €35/h fully loaded works approximately 1,607 hours per year. 40% of that time is 643 hours. At €35/h, that's **€22,505/year per staff member** spent on tasks a machine can handle with equal quality — often better, since it doesn't suffer from fatigue-related errors.

For a firm with 5 staff members, we're talking about **€112,525/year** of productive capacity locked into mechanical work.

This is not inevitable. Here are the 5 tasks to automate first.

## 5 tasks to automate immediately

### 1. Automated payment reminders

Chasing clients for late payments is time-consuming and often postponed. The result: cash flow deteriorates, unpaid invoices pile up.

An automated workflow sends reminders at **D+3** (polite reminder), **D+15** (firm follow-up) and **D+30** (formal notice), without human intervention. Staff only steps in for disputed cases.

**Tools**: n8n, Make, or Zapier connected to your invoicing software.

**Measured gain**: 8 to 12 hours/month per staff member, collection rate improved by 25 to 40%.

For a deeper dive on this topic, read our [complete guide to automating payment reminders](/blog/comptable-automatiser-relances-impayees).

### 2. AI-powered OCR bookkeeping

Manual invoice entry is the most time-consuming task in any firm. OCR solutions (optical character recognition) coupled with AI automatically extract data from invoices: amount, date, supplier, invoice number, VAT.

**Tools**: Dext (formerly Receipt Bank), AutoEntry, Yooz. These platforms achieve a **95 to 98% accuracy rate** on standardised French invoices.

**How it works**: the client photographs or emails their invoice → OCR extracts the data → the software pre-assigns the accounting entry → the staff member validates with one click.

**Measured gain**: 15 to 20 hours/month per staff member. Processing time drops from 3-5 minutes per invoice to 15 seconds of validation.

### 3. Automatic bank reconciliation

Matching bank statements line by line is dead time. New-generation accounting software connected to Open Banking feeds performs reconciliation automatically.

**Tools**: Pennylane, Inqom (formerly iPaidThat Pro), Sage Automated Accounting. These platforms connect directly to banks via PSD2 APIs and suggest matches with a **90 to 95% correspondence rate**.

**How it works**: bank transactions arrive in real time → AI matches them to previously entered invoices → staff validates proposed associations and only handles anomalies.

**Measured gain**: 10 to 15 hours/month per staff member. Monthly reconciliation for a client file drops from 45 minutes to 5 minutes.

### 4. Automated VAT pre-returns

Preparing VAT returns involves collecting, verifying and aggregating data scattered across multiple files. It's a mechanical process but carries high responsibility.

Automation doesn't replace the accountant on final validation — it prepares the groundwork.

**How it works**: accounting entries are automatically categorised by VAT rate → the system generates a draft return → the accountant checks amounts, special cases (reverse charge, intra-community VAT) and validates.

**Tools**: Pennylane, Cegid Loop, ACD (declarative module). These platforms integrate French tax rules and generate drafts compliant with the EDI format expected by the French tax authority (DGFiP).

**Measured gain**: 5 to 8 hours/month per staff member. Data entry error risk reduced by 70%.

### 5. Automated client reporting

Your clients expect dashboards, monthly summaries, performance indicators. Preparing these documents manually takes considerable time — yet 80% of the content is identical from one month to the next.

**How it works**: accounting data is extracted automatically → a report template is populated with the month's figures → the document is generated as a PDF → it's sent to the client by email on a fixed date.

**Tools**: Finthesis, MyReport (Report One group), or an n8n/Make workflow connected to your accounting software and a layout tool (Google Slides API, Carbone.io).

**Measured gain**: 6 to 10 hours/month. Each client report goes from 30-45 minutes of preparation to a simple 2-minute validation.

## Where to start when you have no technical skills

You don't need to be a developer to automate your firm. Here's the roadmap.

### Step 1: Identify your 3 most time-consuming tasks

For one week, ask each staff member to log time spent on repetitive tasks. Rank them by volume. Typically, bookkeeping entry and bank reconciliation come out on top.

### Step 2: Test a SaaS tool for 30 days

Choose a tool suited to your priority task. Dext for bookkeeping, Pennylane for bank reconciliation. All offer free trials or accessible entry pricing.

Don't migrate the entire firm at once. Test on 5 to 10 representative client files. Measure the actual time saved.

### Step 3: Automate workflows between tools

Once SaaS tools are in place, connect them with automation platforms like [n8n](https://n8n.io) (open source, self-hostable) or [Make](https://make.com). For example: "When Dext validates an invoice → create the entry in Pennylane → update the client tracking spreadsheet."

## Frequently asked questions

### Will AI replace accountants?

No. AI automates mechanical tasks (data entry, reconciliation, reminders). It does not replace human expertise: tax advice, wealth optimisation, strategic guidance, client relationships. Firms that automate don't cut positions — they reposition their staff on higher-value missions.

### Is my client data safe with these tools?

The platforms mentioned (Dext, Pennylane, Finthesis) are hosted in France or Europe, GDPR-compliant, and certified for processing accounting data. Always check certifications (ISO 27001, SOC 2) and data processing agreements before signing.
