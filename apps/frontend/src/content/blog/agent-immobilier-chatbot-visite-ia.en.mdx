## The real cost of unqualified viewings

According to a 2024 NAR (National Association of Realtors) study, a real estate agent conducts an average of **48 property viewings per month**. The average conversion rate to signed listings is **8.3%**. That means 4 deals for 48 viewings.

But more importantly: **37 to 42% of those viewings** involve prospects who aren't ready to buy. No financing plan, unrealistic budget, curiosity without commitment. A field agent loses between **15 and 20 hours per month** on viewings that will never close.

At a loaded hourly cost of €75 (travel, preparation, energy), that's between **€1,125 and €1,500/month** of wasted time. Per agent.

## How an AI chatbot qualifies a real estate prospect

A qualification chatbot doesn't make small talk. It asks the right questions in the right order, and decides whether the prospect deserves your time.

### The 5 qualification questions

1. **What type of property are you looking for?** (Apartment, house, land, commercial space)
2. **In which area?** (City, neighborhood, radius in km/miles)
3. **What is your maximum budget?** (With guided range, not a free text field)
4. **Have you already obtained financing approval?** (Yes/No/In progress — the #1 filter question)
5. **What is your purchase timeline?** (< 3 months / 3-6 months / > 6 months / "just browsing")

The chatbot assigns a **qualification score** based on the answers. A prospect with validated financing, a budget aligned with the local market, and a timeline under 3 months gets priority. A "just browsing, no financing" gets a polite response with a link to a mortgage simulator — not a viewing.

### What happens in practice

**Qualified prospect (score > 70/100):**
1. The chatbot directly offers a viewing slot via Calendly or Cal.com
2. The prospect picks their time
3. The agent receives a notification with the full profile (budget, financing, property type, area)
4. The viewing is automatically confirmed by SMS

**Unqualified prospect (score < 40/100):**
1. The chatbot thanks them politely
2. It sends a free guide ("7 steps to prepare your property purchase")
3. The prospect is added to an email nurturing sequence
4. When they secure financing, they come back — and this time they're qualified

**Mid-range prospect (score 40-70):**
1. The chatbot offers a 10-minute phone call (not a viewing)
2. The agent confirms qualification by phone
3. If validated, the viewing is scheduled

## FAQ

### Is a chatbot on my real estate website GDPR-compliant?

Yes, as long as you follow 3 rules: display a consent banner before data collection, store data on European servers (or with standard contractual clauses), and allow data deletion on request. European SaaS platforms (Crisp, Landbot) are compliant by default. A custom chatbot hosted in France or the EU is as well.

### Can the chatbot integrate with my listings on Zillow or Realtor.com?

Not directly. Zillow and Realtor.com don't provide public APIs for individual agents. However, the chatbot lives on your website — and your website is where you redirect prospects from your listings. The flow becomes: listing on Zillow → your website → chatbot → qualification → qualified viewing.

### What real qualification rate can I expect?

On implementations I've built for real estate agents in southern France, the qualification rate (prospects who complete the chatbot journey and pass the threshold) is **62 to 74%** of interactions. Of those who pass the threshold, **83%** actually show up for the viewing.

### How long does it take to set up the chatbot?

A SaaS like Tidio or Crisp is operational in 2-3 hours. A custom chatbot with scoring and CRM integration takes 5 to 10 business days. The longest part isn't the development — it's defining with you the qualification criteria suited to your local market.
