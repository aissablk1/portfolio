You want an AI chatbot for your business. You search "how much does an AI chatbot cost" on Google. You find vague answers: "it depends on your needs", "contact us for a quote."

Here are the real prices. No filter.

## The 3 categories of AI chatbots and their real prices

### Option 1: Turnkey SaaS (€30 to €300/month)

Platforms like Tidio, Crisp, Intercom, or Drift offer chatbots you can integrate in a few clicks.

**Actual 2026 prices:**
| Platform | AI chatbot included | Price/month |
|----------|-------------------|-------------|
| Tidio | Yes (Lyro AI) | €29 |
| Crisp | Yes (basic) | €95 |
| Intercom | Yes (Fin AI) | €74 + €0.99/resolution |
| Drift | Yes | From €250 |

**What you get:**
- Working chatbot in 1-2 hours
- Trained on your FAQ or website
- Simple management interface
- Technical support included

**The limitations:**
- Generic responses, not always relevant to your industry
- Limited visual customization
- No integration with your business tools (CRM, ERP)
- Data hosted by the provider (GDPR to verify)
- Cumulative cost: €360 to €3,600/year — without owning anything

**For whom:** Small businesses wanting a basic FAQ chatbot, quickly, without development budget.

### Option 2: Custom freelance (€1,500 to €5,000)

A specialized developer creates a chatbot trained on your data, integrated with your tools, matching your branding.

**Actual 2026 prices:**
| Scope | Fixed price | Timeline |
|-------|------------|----------|
| Simple FAQ chatbot (website) | €1,500 | 5 days |
| Lead qualification chatbot | €2,500 | 7 days |
| Chatbot + CRM + notifications | €2,900 | 10 days |
| Advanced chatbot (multi-language, workflow) | €4,000 - €5,000 | 2-3 weeks |

**What you get:**
- Chatbot trained on YOUR data (FAQ, catalog, pricing)
- Integration with your existing tools (CRM, email, calendar)
- Custom design (your branding)
- You own the code and data
- Optional maintenance after delivery

**The limitations:**
- Requires a clear brief from you
- Maintenance to plan (€490/month on average)
- Depends on the freelancer's competence

**For whom:** SMBs wanting a truly useful chatbot, integrated into their workflow, that they own.

### Option 3: Agency (€10,000 to €50,000)

A digital agency designs, develops, and maintains a chatbot as part of a global project (website + chatbot + CRM + analytics).

**Actual 2026 prices:**
| Scope | Price | Timeline |
|-------|-------|----------|
| Chatbot integrated with a showcase site | €10,000 - €15,000 | 2-3 months |
| Chatbot + CRM + analytics | €15,000 - €25,000 | 3-4 months |
| Complete conversational platform | €25,000 - €50,000 | 4-6 months |

**What you get:**
- Dedicated team (project manager, developer, designer)
- Elaborate conversational strategy
- User testing and iterations
- Maintenance and evolutions included (usually 1 year)

**The limitations:**
- High minimum budget
- Long timelines
- Dependency on the agency for modifications
- Often oversized for an SMB

**For whom:** Mid-size or large companies with a substantial digital budget and complex needs.

## How to choose?

**Choose SaaS if:**
- You need a simple FAQ chatbot
- You don't have a development budget
- You want to test the concept before investing

**Choose custom freelance if:**
- You want a chatbot that actually qualifies your prospects
- You need integration with your tools (CRM, calendar)
- You want to own the solution

**Choose an agency if:**
- Your project is complex (multi-language, multi-channel, high volume)
- You have the budget and time
- You need a dedicated team

## FAQ

### Does an AI chatbot replace a human customer service?

No. A good AI chatbot handles 60 to 80% of recurring questions (hours, pricing, availability, processes). Complex or sensitive questions are redirected to a human. The goal isn't to replace — it's to free up time for the requests that matter.

### Can my chatbot give wrong answers?

Yes, that's a risk. A SaaS chatbot trained on generic data can fabricate answers. A custom chatbot trained on your data with safeguards (responses limited to scope, escalation to human when in doubt) significantly reduces this risk.

### Should I have a chatbot on every page?

No. Strategic pages are enough: homepage, contact page, service pages. On blog articles or legal pages, the chatbot is unnecessary and intrusive.
