You're an insurance broker. Your phone rings 30 times a day. Out of those 30 calls, how many result in a signed contract? Two? Three? The rest is time wasted explaining coverages to people who are shopping around with no intention of buying.

This problem has a solution. And it doesn't cost €50,000.

## The problem: 60% of your calls lead nowhere

An independent broker spends on average 60% of their sales time on prospects who will never sign. Here are the profiles you know by heart:

**The chronic comparison shoppers.** They call 8 brokers, request 8 quotes, and end up buying the cheapest option online. You spent 45 minutes for nothing.

**Off-target requests.** Someone looking for builders' insurance when you specialize in health coverage. You politely redirect them — after a 15-minute conversation.

**Basic questions.** "What's the difference between health insurance and supplemental coverage?" You become a free call center instead of selling.

**The real cost:** out of 5 appointments booked in a typical week, only 1 is truly qualified. The other 4 are sales time you'll never get back. At €80/hour of sales time (a conservative figure for a broker), those 4 wasted appointments cost you €640/week. That's **€2,560/month of wasted time**.

The question isn't "can I afford to automate?" It's "can I afford not to?"

## The 3-part system

The idea isn't to replace your expertise — nobody signs an insurance contract with a robot. The idea is to filter before you step in, so every minute of your time is invested on a prospect with a real chance of signing.

### Part 1: the pre-qualification chatbot on your website

A smart chatbot on your website asks 5 key questions before passing anything to you:

1. **Type of insurance needed** — Business, health, life, auto, home? This lets you immediately filter off-target requests.
2. **Expected monthly budget** — Under €50, €50-150, over €150? A prospect with a €20/month budget for family health coverage isn't worth 45 minutes of your time.
3. **Current situation** — Already insured and looking to switch? New need? Current contract expiry date? A prospect whose contract expires in 30 days is red-hot.
4. **Decision timeline** — This week, this month, "just looking"? The "just looking" crowd gets routed to your automated FAQ.
5. **Contact details** — Name, phone, email. No contact details, no follow-up.

The chatbot runs 24/7. A prospect who visits your website at 11 PM on a Sunday is captured and qualified while you sleep.

### Part 2: the automated CRM that scores and routes

The chatbot responses automatically feed into your CRM. Each lead receives a score:

| Score | Profile | Automated action |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| **Hot** (80-100) | Matching budget, immediate need, upcoming expiry | Instant notification + appointment offer |
| **Warm** (40-79) | Real need but not urgent, budget to confirm | Automated nurturing email, follow-up at D+3 |
| **Cold** (0-39) | Comparison shopper, off-target, no budget | Automated FAQ, no sales time spent |

Routing is automatic: if you have an associate who specializes in business insurance while you handle health, leads are directed to the right person with no manual intervention.

### Part 3: automatic appointment booking

For hot leads only, the system directly offers a slot in your calendar via a tool like Calendly. The prospect picks their slot, receives a confirmation with SMS reminder, and you find the appointment in your calendar along with the complete prospect file.

No more email back-and-forth to find a time slot. No more hot prospects going cold over 3 days waiting for your response.

## Frequently asked questions

### Will my prospects agree to talk to a chatbot?

Yes. 73% of consumers prefer a chatbot for simple questions rather than waiting on hold (source: Tidio, 2025). The chatbot doesn't replace your expertise — it asks 5 questions in 2 minutes so your expertise is deployed at the right time, with the right prospect.

### Is this GDPR compliant?

Yes, provided you respect the basics: explicit consent, data processing information, right to deletion, hosting in Europe. A custom chatbot hosted in France is the safest option. American SaaS platforms (Tidio, HubSpot) offer compliant options, but check the data transfer clauses.

### I'm not technical — can I set this up myself?

The SaaS option (Tidio + HubSpot + Calendly) is doable on your own in 1-2 days with online tutorials. The custom option requires a developer — but it's a 7-10 day project, not a 6-month endeavor.
