Tuesday, 6:15 p.m. Sophie is closing her salon on rue Timbal, in central Albi. She has spent the day between the basin, the dryer, and the till — and has let her landline ring seven times because her hands were covered in colour. At closing, she listens to her voicemail: three booking requests, two clients cancelling tomorrow, a supplier following up. She calls everyone back between 6:30 and 7:15 p.m., writes the appointments into her paper diary, and realises she has double-booked the 10 a.m. slot on Thursday.

The next morning, 9:45 a.m.: the 10 a.m. client is a no-show. No text, no answer on the phone. Sophie had blocked 1h30 for a 95 € highlights service she will never invoice. She does have a website — redesigned two years ago — with a visible “Book now” button at the top of the page. Except that nobody uses it. She herself no longer really knows how it works, and she never activated SMS reminders because she was “afraid they would cost too much”.

Sophie is not an edge case. She is the norm.

## Decorative booking, or the illusion of being digital

France Num, the French public programme supporting the digital transition of small and mid-sized businesses, notes in its sector studies that local service businesses — including hair salons and beauty institutes — massively equip their shop windows with digital tools (websites, Google Business profiles, booking buttons) without actually using their management features. The difference between a salon that survives and one that grows is not whether a booking module exists. It is whether that module is woven into the daily life of the team.

Field observations in independent salons consistently show a no-show range between 5 and 20 % of appointments, depending on the neighbourhood, the clientele, the service mix, and whether automatic reminders are in place. That is huge. On a full schedule, it is the equivalent of half a day lost every week.

Three things genuinely separate a decorative agenda from a system that earns its keep:

1. **Automatic reminders** — SMS or email 24 to 48 hours before, with one-click cancellation
2. **A historised client card** — colour formula used, processing time, allergies, last visit, average ticket
3. **Proactive re-engagement** — following up clients who haven’t booked in X weeks

Without those three bricks, the booking widget is just a button on a page. With them, it becomes the engine of your business.

## The hidden cost of the status quo

Take Sophie’s case: a 2-chair salon in Albi, 120 visits a week, 48 € average ticket.

**Cost of phone time:**
- Roughly 35 incoming calls a week (bookings, changes, pricing questions)
- Average call length: 3 minutes
- Total: 1h45 per week, roughly **91 hours a year**
- Valued at the average hourly cost of an experienced hairdresser (roughly 32 €/h, charges included): **≈ 2,900 €/year**

**Cost of no-shows:**
- Conservative assumption: 8 % of appointments missed, i.e. around 10 slots a week
- Average lost billable slot: 40 € (mix of cuts, colours, blow-dries)
- Total: 400 €/week × 48 working weeks = **≈ 19,200 €/year**

**Cost of double bookings and forgotten appointments:**
- 1 to 2 scheduling mistakes a week — unhappy client, wasted slot, goodwill gesture to smooth things over
- Conservative valuation: **≈ 1,500 €/year**

**Total hidden cost: around 23,000 €/year** for a two-chair salon. It is not a bill you receive — it is revenue that never shows up on your statements. And that is precisely why you don’t see it.

## The 3 levels of online booking

### Level 1 — Shared Google Calendar: free

The simplest option: a Google Workspace calendar shared across the team, viewable on smartphones, with a basic booking link via Google (the “Appointments” feature in Google Workspace Business) or a generic tool like Calendly (free up to one appointment type).

**What it does:**
- Real-time shared visibility for the whole team
- Eliminates paper-diary double bookings
- Automatic sync on every hairdresser’s phone
- A public link that clients can use to book on their own, 24/7

**What it doesn’t do:**
- No automatic SMS reminders (only email, which beauty clients rarely read)
- No historised client card (no memory of colour formulas, processing times, last visit)
- No handling of variable-duration services (a colour service varies with hair length)
- No deposit collection to secure high-value slots

**For whom:** a solo or two-person salon with a stable, loyal clientele that simply wants to stop keeping a paper book without committing financially. A perfect first step.

**Setup:** 2 to 3 hours of configuration. Free if you already have a Gmail address. 6 to 12 €/month per user if you move to Google Workspace Business for the advanced features.

### Level 2 — Specialised beauty SaaS platform: 30 to 90 €/month

This is the French market standard. Planity, the French beauty booking platform, and Treatwell, its international counterpart with a strong French presence, dominate the segment. Other specialised beauty SaaS platforms exist and can be compared — the key is to choose one that bundles SMS reminders, client cards, and multi-chair management.

**What it does:**
- Online booking from your site, from your listing in the platform’s directory, and from your Google Business Profile
- Automatic SMS reminders 24 to 48 hours before (included or billed per SMS depending on the plan)
- Basic client card: visit history, services, average ticket
- Multi-chair and multi-service management with variable durations
- Deposit collection at booking for longer services
- Accounting export, occupancy statistics, fill-rate tracking

**What it doesn’t do:**
- Your clientele is shared with the platform’s directory — which can bring new clients, but also puts you in direct competition with the salon next door inside the same app
- You don’t fully own the client data — if you leave, visit histories don’t always come with you
- Limited customisation of the booking flow beyond the platform’s templates
- Some marketing automation features (birthdays, smart follow-ups) are basic or paid add-ons

**For whom:** salons with 1 to 5 chairs that want a turnkey tool, benefit from directory-driven traffic, and accept playing by the platform’s rules.

**Setup:** 1 to 2 weeks to configure services, schedules, team, and reminders. Subscription between 30 and 90 €/month depending on the platform and options. No setup fee in most cases.

### Level 3 — Custom site + client card + marketing automation: 3,000 to 7,000 € then 20 €/month

This is the option for salons that want to build a **brand**, not just slot themselves into a directory. A custom site with integrated booking, a full client card, and retention automations turns your business into an actual management system.

**What it does:**
- Booking directly on your site, in your visual identity, with no redirect to a third party
- Full client card: colour history (formulas, processing times, brands used), allergies, before/after photos, preferences, stylist notes
- SMS and email reminders branded with your name, not the platform’s
- Automatic follow-ups to clients who haven’t booked in 6, 8 or 10 weeks depending on their usual cadence
- Automated birthday messages with targeted offers
- Newsletter to showcase a new collection, a new brand, or an event
- Single dashboard: occupancy, revenue per stylist, no-show rate, average ticket, top services
- Well-tuned local SEO (“hair salon central Albi”, “blonde balayage Albi”) to capture Google searches
- You own the client data, forever

**What it doesn’t do:**
- Doesn’t maintain itself — you still need to publish some content and keep client cards up to date
- Not a short-term investment: ROI typically shows over 12 to 18 months
- Not suited for a salon that still hesitates to fill a paper diary — a real will to drive the business is required

**For whom:** established salons with a strong identity, looking to move beyond directory logic, actively retain clients, and build over 3 to 5 years. Or a beauty institute combining hair and skin care that needs a rich client card.

**Setup:** 4 to 8 weeks. Design cost between 3,000 and 7,000 € depending on modules, then 15 to 30 €/month for hosting and maintenance, plus SMS billed at actual cost (around 0.06 € per SMS).

## Traps to avoid

**Trap 1 — The “Book now” button that leads nowhere.** Many salons display a visible button that actually redirects to a contact form or, worse, to a phone number. From the client’s perspective, that is the same as no button at all. If you show a booking button, it must trigger a real slot reservation in under 30 seconds, with no account creation.

**Trap 2 — SMS reminders disabled “to save money”.** An SMS costs roughly 0.06 €. A no-show costs you 40 to 100 €. Disabling reminders to save 20 €/month when they would prevent 15 no-shows is the worst possible math. Always keep them on, 24 to 48 hours before, with a cancel link that automatically frees up the slot.

**Trap 3 — Letting the platform own the client relationship.** When you go exclusively through a directory, your clients become the platform’s clients. The day it raises prices, tweaks its algorithm, or promotes a competitor, you are exposed. Use a platform for acquisition — but systematically collect phone and email, and build your own database in parallel.

**Trap 4 — Underestimating the client card.** The real retention lever in a salon is neither price nor promotions — it is memory. A client who comes back and hears “shall we go with your usual 7.43 like last time?” never leaves. A client you have to ask five questions she has already answered, will. A historised client card is what separates a service from a relationship.

## How much does it really cost

Realistic ranges for a 1-to-5-chair salon in 2026:

| Item | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Setup | 0 to 100 € | 0 to 300 € | 3,000 to 7,000 € |
| Monthly subscription | 0 to 12 € | 30 to 90 € | 15 to 30 € |
| Reminder SMS | not included | included or 0.06 €/SMS | 0.06 €/SMS |
| Weekly management time | 2 to 3 h | 30 min to 1 h | 15 to 30 min |
| No-show reduction | low | strong | strong + targeted follow-ups |
| Ownership of client data | full | partial | full |

**Level 3 break-even:** if you recover just **10 slots a month** that would otherwise have been lost to no-shows or forgotten bookings (about 400 €/month in recovered revenue), you amortise a 5,000 € site in 12 to 14 months. And that is without counting the time freed up from phone calls, or the clients retained by automatic follow-ups.
