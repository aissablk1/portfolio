Your clients no longer just type into Google. They open ChatGPT, Perplexity or Gemini and ask the question directly: *"Which provider should I contact to digitize my business?"*

If your website doesn't appear in those answers, you're invisible to a growing share of your audience.

## SEO is no longer enough — welcome to the GEO era

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) optimizes your site for traditional search engines: Google, Bing. You work on keywords, backlinks, page speed. It's still essential.

GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) adds a layer: making your content **understandable and citable by generative AI**. These AIs don't work like Google. They don't rank pages — they synthesize answers from sources they deem reliable and relevant.

**The fundamental difference:**

| | SEO | GEO |
|---|---|---|
| **Target** | Google, Bing | ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Copilot |
| **Goal** | Appear in the top 10 results | Be cited in the generated answer |
| **Criteria** | Keywords, backlinks, speed | Demonstrated expertise, structured data, consistency |
| **Winning format** | Pages optimized per keyword | Authority content with clear semantic structure |
| **Measurement** | SERP position | Mention in AI responses |

## What AI looks for to cite you

Language models don't crawl the web like Googlebot. They rely on:

### 1. Positioning consistency

If your website says "automation expert", your LinkedIn says "full-stack developer" and your Google listing says "digital consultant", the AI doesn't know what to retain. A clear, repeated positioning across all your channels increases your chances of being cited.

### 2. Structured data (Schema.org)

JSON-LD tags allow AI to understand **who you are**, **what you do** and **where you operate**. Without them, your site is a block of text. With them, it's a machine-readable identity card.

Essential schemas:
- `LocalBusiness`: name, address, service area
- `ProfessionalService`: service type, pricing, expertise
- `FAQPage`: frequently asked questions with direct answers
- `Article`: author, date, topic — for your blog content

### 3. Authority content

AIs favor sources that:
- **Answer a question directly** (no vague or marketing content)
- **Cite concrete numbers** (costs, timelines, ROI)
- **Demonstrate specific expertise** (case studies, methodologies)
- **Are regularly updated** (recent dates)

### 4. Consistent multi-channel presence

Your website alone isn't enough. AIs cross-reference sources: website, LinkedIn, Google Business Profile, professional directories, blog articles, client reviews. The more consistent these sources are, the more credible you appear to the AI.

## 5 concrete actions to appear in AI responses

### Action 1: Structure your data with Schema.org

Add JSON-LD tags to your site. It's invisible to visitors but essential for AI. A website without structured data in 2026 is like a resume without a name.

### Action 2: Create thematic expertise pages

One page per service, with: the problem solved, the method, concrete results, pricing. AIs love content that answers "who to contact for [problem] in [city]".

### Action 3: Align all your channels

Same positioning, same keywords, same expertise on your website, LinkedIn, Google Business, and every directory where you're listed.

### Action 4: Publish high-value content

Blog articles that answer the questions your clients ask AI. Not hollow SEO content stuffed with keywords — content that demonstrates your expertise with numbers and examples.

### Action 5: Update regularly

A website with 2023 dates will be ignored by AIs looking for recent information. Update pricing, case studies, and articles at least quarterly.

## Does GEO replace SEO?

No. GEO complements SEO. You need both:
- **SEO** to be found on Google (still 85% of search traffic)
- **GEO** to be cited in AI responses (growing rapidly)

Best practices largely overlap: quality content, clear structure, up-to-date data. The difference is the addition of structured data and extra rigor on positioning consistency.

## FAQ

### How do I know if AI cites me?

Ask the question yourself on ChatGPT, Perplexity and Gemini: *"Which [your profession] should I contact in [your city]?"*. If you don't appear, there's work to do.

### Do AIs use Google reviews?

Yes. Google reviews are a credibility source that AIs take into account. A Google Business profile with 20+ recent, well-rated reviews significantly increases your chances of being recommended.

### How long before seeing results?

Structured data and multi-channel alignment take effect within a few weeks. Authority content takes 2 to 3 months to be indexed and considered by AIs.
