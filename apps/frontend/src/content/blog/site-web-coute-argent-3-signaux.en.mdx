You're paying for hosting. You paid someone to build your website. Maybe even a premium domain name.

And yet, your site generates no calls, no emails, no quote requests.

That's not normal. A professional website is an investment — not an expense. If it's not generating returns, there's an identifiable, fixable problem.

Here are the 3 signs your website is costing you money instead of making it.

## Sign 1: You don't know how many visitors you get

If you can't answer "how many people visit my site each month?", you're flying blind.

**The concrete problem:**
- No Google Analytics installed, or installed but never checked
- No idea which pages get visited
- No way to know if your marketing investments generate traffic

**What it costs:**
A site without analytics is a store without a foot traffic counter. You can't measure or improve. You keep paying (hosting, maintenance, domain) without knowing if it's working.

**The fix:**
Installing Google Analytics 4 takes 15 minutes. Setting up a basic dashboard with essential metrics (visitors, page views, traffic sources) takes one hour. Cost: zero. Return: you finally know where you stand.

## Sign 2: Your last inbound contact via the site is over 3 months old

A professional website has one job: generate qualified contacts. If your last call, email, or quote request from the site is over 3 months old, your site is a dead storefront.

**Common causes:**
- Contact form hidden at the bottom of the page
- No clear call-to-action (no visible "Get a quote" button)
- The site doesn't answer the visitor's question: "why you instead of someone else?"
- No phone number visible on mobile
- The site takes more than 3 seconds to load — 53% of mobile visitors leave after that

**What it costs:**
Take a contractor billing €45/h. One lost lead per week due to an ineffective site means one lost project per month. On an average €3,000 project, that's **€36,000/year in uncaptured revenue**.

**The fix:**
- Place a CTA above the fold
- Display a clickable phone number on mobile
- Add a smart contact form that qualifies the need
- Reduce load time under 2 seconds

## Sign 3: Your contact form sends an email nobody reads

The prospect takes the time to fill out your form. Describes their need. Waits for a response.

And they get... nothing. No confirmation. No callback within 24h. No automated follow-up.

**What actually happens:**
- The notification email lands in spam
- Nobody is responsible for processing inbound requests
- The prospect contacts a competitor who responds in 2 hours

**The numbers:**
- 78% of prospects sign with the first provider who responds
- Average response time for SMBs: 42 hours
- Companies that respond within one hour convert 7x more

**The fix:**
- Immediate automatic confirmation email ("Thank you, we'll get back to you within 24h")
- Real-time notification on your phone when a prospect fills the form
- Automatic follow-up sequence at D+3 if you haven't responded
- Tracking dashboard to never lose a lead again

## FAQ

### My site was redone less than a year ago, can these signs still apply?

Yes. A recent but poorly designed site (no CTA, basic form, no analytics) has exactly the same problems. Creation date has nothing to do with effectiveness.

### I don't have the budget for a full redesign, where should I start?

With sign 1: install Google Analytics. It's free and gives you the data to decide next steps. Then fix sign 3 (notifications) — it's the most immediately profitable.

### Do these problems also apply to Wix/WordPress sites?

Yes, regardless of the technology. These signs are about design and strategy, not the platform.
